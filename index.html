What is a palindrome?

A palindrome is a word, phrase, number, or sequence of characters that reads the same forward and backward, ignoring spaces, punctuation, and capitalization.

Palindromes exhibit symmetry, making them an interesting concept in language and mathematics.

Examples of Palindromes

  • Words
    • madam
    • radar
    • rotor
  • Phrases
    • A man, a plan, a canal, Panama! (Ignoring spaces, punctuation, and capitalization: "amanaplanacanalpanama")
    • Was it a car or a cat I saw? (When simplified: "wasitacaroracatisaw")
